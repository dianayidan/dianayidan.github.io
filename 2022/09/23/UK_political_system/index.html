How is UK's Political system and business customs?

Britain is a constitutional monarchy governed by a king or a queen and it is also a parliamentary democracy. whose government is controlled by a parliament that has been elected by the people. The highest positions in the government are filled by the members of the directly elected parliament.

The British political system is a two-party system. Since the 1920s, the two dominant parties have been the Conservative Party and the Labour Party. In September of 2022, Liz Truss became the new prime minister of the UK after meeting the queen at Balmoral. Her first significant act as prime minister is to deal with the high price of energy and made it affordable to the people of the UK.

For political stability, we can look at the indicator of the political stability index which ranges from -2.5 (weak) to +2.5 (strong). From the year 1996 to 2020, the average value for the United Kingdom was 0.48 with a minimum of 0.08 points in 2018 and a maximum of 1.04 points in 2000. The latest value from 2020 is 0.47 and compared to the world average value based on 194 countries -0.07. [1]Which can indicate that the UK has a relatively stable government which performed better than the average level in the world. From these data, we can get a general conclusion of the actual situation that the British government is governing the whole country.

As for the special taxes in the UK, there are carbon tax, sugar tax, and petrol tax. A Carbon tax aims to make individuals and firms pay the full social cost of carbon pollution. This special tax will encourage individuals to reduce pollution and encourage them to use more environmentally products such as solar power. It also makes polluters pay the external cost of carbon emissions. In contrast, there also exist some problems, for example, enterprises may avoid paying carbon tax by secretly polluting the greenhouse gases into the environment.[2] Under this scenario, the government should strengthen the education on ethical behavior and set guidelines and regulations for the people to follow. Consumption of sugar imposes costs on individuals such as resulting in lower life expectancy. [3]Imposing a sugar tax on the product can reduce the consumption of unhealthy food and guide people to live healthier lives. It also encourages firms to produce healthier food and beverages. Besides that, it also helps the government to raise revenue and have higher spending on the health care sector. However, increasing the tax may still cause negative effects such as tax evasion and an increase in the living cost of the lower income family. In terms of the petrol cost, a higher petrol tax could help improve struggling government tax revenues and also contribute to a better environment and lower congestion levels. [4] There are also very strong environmental and health benefits from reducing the reliance on the car and petrol tax could play a role in this. However, we must also pay attention to the price of the electric cars when there is exceeding demand, it will drive down the market price of the cars which then lowers the manufacturers’ willingness to produce the cars. Intellectual property usually gives the creator an exclusive right over the use of his creation for a certain period. Copyright, patents, designs, and trademarks are common rights for intellectual property protection. The 2022 International IP Index published by the US Chamber of Commerce has given out the rankings and the indicators of the overall score of IP. The UK has ranked No.2 out of the 55 economies. It has a high ranking in the sector of copyright and trademark and a relatively lower ranking in patents and design rights.[8] Copyright law originated in the United Kingdom from a concept of common law which is the Statute of Anne 1709. It became statutory with the passing of the Copyright Act 1911. The current act is the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988.[9] Also, 179 countries have signed up to the Berne Convention which lays down a common framework and agreement between nations concerning intellectual property rights.

Before the Industrial Revolution British society was divided into groups depending on what kind of family you were born into, which then influenced your occupation, social status, and political influence. However, this has changed due to the fact more people are receiving higher education, especially at the university level. The British class system can mainly be divided into five layers.[11] The lower class is the long-term unemployed homeless people. The working class owns the basic low-level unskilled or semi-skilled workers, such as those with no college education. This includes occupations such as factory workers and laborers. The middle class is a majority of the British people including the white-collar professionals, teachers, journalists, and nurses. Next is about the upper class and a barrister can be a good consideration. Finally, which is the aristocrats like the royal family and those with titles, such as lords or barons. The most popular view of society was Type B which is a society like a pyramid with a small elite at the top, more people in the middle, and most at the bottom.[12] Working class identity remains widespread in Britain. Even though only a minority of people are engaged in working-class occupations, the majority of the people still think of themselves as working class. The current sources of working-class identity amongst those in middle-class jobs may now be beginning to dry up.
