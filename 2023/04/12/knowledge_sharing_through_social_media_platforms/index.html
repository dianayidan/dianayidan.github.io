Can knowledge sharing through social media platforms impact the wealth management behavior of Chinese citizens?

Abstract:

- Purpose: Thus the objective of this report is to discover if knowledge sharing through social media platforms impacts the wealth management behavior of Chinese citizens.

- Design/methodology/approach: A questionaire is being designed and b*oth quantitative and qualitative methods will be used to analyze the influential factors that influence users‚Äô behavior on social media about wealth management.*

- Findings: The collected data in the questionnaires which finds out that the level of trust in the influencers is the most influential factor.

- Originality/value: This paper will demonstrate ways of maintaining trust in the virtual community and how to enhance engagement in social media such as Dou yin.

- Practical implications: Knowledge sharing through social media platforms will have a positive impact on wealth management behavior after acquiring knowledge from the bank customer‚Äôs manager and the financial institutions.

- Social implications: This paper aims to enhance the investment awareness of the Chinese citizens by promoting knowledge sharing about wealth management on social media such as Dou yin.

Keywords:

knowledge sharing, knowledge management, communist of practice, virtual community, Influencer

1 Introduction

In recent decades, economic development changes the demographic structure. The process of urbanization causes residents‚Äô income to rise. The demand for household financial management has further accelerated.[1], [2]. Nowadays many Chinese citizens choose to put their money in a bank account to avoid taking risks[3]. China‚Äôs financial markets are quite primitive compared with the financial markets in the United States and Europe. In an annual report published by the Shanghai Stock Exchange, there are 85% of daily trading comes from retail investors in contrast institutional investors only contribute 15% of the trading volume [4]. Most of the financial contributions come from retail investors in China. Thus, it is important to enhance the investment awareness of the individuals and find channels to promote knowledge sharing about financial management. Affected by the COVID-19 epidemic, online entertainment demand continues to increase driving the scale of users of audio-visual applications a further growth[5]. Social media can promote knowledge sharing to the public and over 40% of users mention that they have no issue with product placements in commercial content or even in the user-generated tutorial video[6]. So social media is an appropriate tool to share different people‚Äôs ideas and opinions and Dou yin will be the chosen platform in this report.

1.1 Take Dou yin as an example of knowledge sharing

Dou yin is a short-video social which is founded and operated by Byte Dance in mainland China. [7] Since 2017, the scale of users has grown rapidly. Daily active users exceed 600 million[8]. Sitting on a huge number of users also with high user frequency and long user duration. Thus, many individuals and companies will choose this platform for interaction. Besides, there are also e-commerce platforms on Dou yin. According to the geographical distribution map of Dou yin user portraits newly released by Baidu Index, it is not difficult to see that Dou yin users are still mainly young and in middle-aged groups of 20-49. Dou yin accounts for about 45% of users in the first and second-tier cities which is significantly higher than other platforms like Kuai shou in China. The total population of the first and second-tier cities is estimated at 300 million which accounts for around 21% of the total population. Users who have downloaded Dou yin are so attracted to it that they spend more than 70% of their time on this platform, and the rest is used by all other apps. So, understanding how to enhance the engagement in Dou yin during the process of knowledge sharing will be greatly important. Knowledge sharing is the process of an individual‚Äôs knowledge being converted into a form that can be understood and used by other people [9]. It is different from the traditional way of sharing knowledge in the past by texting messages or giving calls. Nowadays knowledge sharing on social media has become a prevalent phenomenon across the world due to its convenience and efficiency, especially among young people [10].

2 Research Framework

2.1 KM Model

For the research framework, the first research model uses the SECI model[11]. It has four knowledge processes and It can be seen as a process of understanding and transforming tacit and explicit knowledge [12]. Tacit knowledge is what people learn from their experience and reflection on the internalization of knowledge. One of its characteristics is that it is hard to articulate and replicate when disseminating it around. According to what Nonaka and Takeuchi proposed, tacit knowledge is the beliefs and intuition that could only be understood and applied by those possessing it. Explicit knowledge can be expressed in different forms such as words or numbers, and it can be stored in technology like the media[13].

The process of transforming tacit to tacit knowledge is called socialization, it happens when people are interacting directly face to face. The knowledge can be learned by observing, imitating, and practicing from other people[14]. Transforming tacit to explicit knowledge needs an intermediary this is the role played by the social media platform. In social media such as Dou yin, users can acquire knowledge from the videos posted by the influencers. This is a process of Externalization. The third phase of the Knowledge management process is a combination of explicit knowledge. In this stage, people can form their community and gather around to share and exchange knowledge with each other[15]. The last phase is called internalization, which is when users absorb and understand the knowledge and can apply it.

2.2 KM Process

Knowledge management processes are the procedures to enable and provide the creation, spreading, and utilizing of knowledge for the benefit of the organization [16]. Thus, the company needs to have the process to achieve its target. In the knowledge management process, the Wiig knowledge management cycle helps to create knowledge and use them in problem-solving or decision-making. There are four phases which are building the knowledge, holding, pooling, and applying the knowledge in life [17].

In the first phase of building knowledge. In Dou yin, users mainly acquire knowledge from videos posted by the KOL or from live streaming. The KOL build their knowledge from formal education and training or their personal experience. Conducting marketing research and posting frequently asked questions are ways to acquire knowledge. In the second stage, knowledge is being held in the tangible forms or individuals to recollect knowledge, accumulate, or add to present knowledge. which is known as holding knowledge.

In stage three Knowledge can be pooled through interactions. Where people of the same communist of practice will gather to share and disseminate knowledge[18]. The last stage in Wiig‚Äôs model is using knowledge. After comprehending the knowledge is it time to apply it in our lives to help us solve problems. Wiig‚Äôs life cycle doesn‚Äôt have to be continuous and carried in a specific order it can be conducted in a parallel and repeated way [19].

The questionnaire contains questions including essential personal information such as age, gender, education level, financial management capital as a percentage of total income, and ways of wealth management.

4.1 Pearson‚Äôs Correlation

The first and most straightforward statistical analysis method is using Pearson‚Äôs correlation. Each of the correlation coefficients r ranges from 0 to 1 for illustrating the level of positive correlations of each independent variable Xi and dependent variable Y. There is a strong positive correlation when r > .7, moderate positive correlation when .7 > r > .3, and weak positive correlation when .3 > r > 0). It is ranges from -1 to 0 for negative correlations of each independent variable Xi and dependent variable Y. There is a strong negative correlation when r < -.7, moderate negative correlation when -.7 < r < -.3, and weak negative correlation when .3 < r < 0. The larger the absolute value of Pearson‚Äôs correlation, the stronger the linear association between the two variables. In our case in the study of the extent to of social media platforms influence the wealth management behavior of Chinese citizens, the independent variable Y is the degree of using financial management sharing on social media in life. The other dependent variable Xi will be considered as a predictor, and we want to reach a conclusion on which factors will have a significant impact on the independent variable Y.

4.2 Stepwise Linear Regression

The stepwise regression model involves adding or removing potential x variables referencing a specific threshold in succession and testing for statistical significance (p-value).

R in a multiple regression analysis is called the coefficient of multiple correlations and shows the relationship between an independent variable and the other dependent variables in a specific model. It ranges from 0 to +1. In the first Model, there is a positive correlation between the level of agreement in gaining effective financial knowledge on social media and using financial knowledge advice on social media in life. It also means that it has a 64.8% chance that will enhance the action of taking financial knowledge sharing on the social media platform. In the Second Model, both levels of agreement in gaining effective financial knowledge on social media and the level of trust in the content creator have a positive correlation with the behavior of using financial knowledge advice on social media in life. It means that 75.9% chances enhance the action of taking financial knowledge shared on social media.

R2 is the coefficient of determination that explains the variations in the dependent variable accounted for by the independent variable. In the first model, an R square of 0.42 means that 42% of the knowledge sharing is accounted for by the level of agreement on social media. In model 2 there is a 57.7% chance provided by dependent variables, namely levels of agreement in gaining effective financial knowledge on social media and the level of trust in the content creator, to enhance the action of taking financial knowledge shared on social media.

Adjusted R2 adjusts the number of independent variables. It will select the new variable added to the model, and each predictor added in will greatly enhance the prediction of the final result. Thus the adjusted R2 gradually increases from model to model 8. The 8 model includes 8 different predictors, and the last model has achieved a value of 0.687 which means that 68.7% of the variation in financial knowledge shared on social media can be explained by the predictors. From this model, we can see that the second influenced factor is the trust in the influencer. When we added it to the model, it can be seen clearly that the adjusted R square has increased from .414 to .568. It is a significant growth compared to other predictors added to the model.

A significant F-change represents how significantly the variables added in have improved the prediction We specified the null hypothesis H0 as the model is not useful in the hypothesis testing and alternative hypothesis Ha as the model is useful. In this case, the 8 values in the 8 models of the significant F change here are all smaller than Œ±=0.05 (significance level) which means we reject the H0 and accept Ha. It can be explained that the 8 models are effective, and the 8 predictors can affect the financial knowledge shared on social media.

5 Qualitative Analysis

In this part, we are going to analyze factors that attract people to engage on social media Platforms like Dou yin. 5.1 Algorithms in Dou yin

First of all, people spend a lot of time on Dou yin because of its powerful AI algorithms. Besides that, the content distribution strategy also plays a key role inside Dou yin. Compared with the ‚Äúselection‚Äù mechanism of traditional Internet products, Byte‚Äôs ‚Äúalgorithm‚Äù can calculate users‚Äô preferences according to their behavior path such as hesitating or rewatching a video. It can then recommend similar videos based on this information.

Thus, users can receive the pushed videos by the system‚Äôs personalized recommendation content automatically. It can save time and energy for the users however, these behaviors will be recorded by Dou yin‚Äôs background program. The longer time the users use Dou yin, the more data they accumulate. By deeply mining these data, Dou yin can determine which content users are more interested in, and then push the content out to what they may like. Dou yin‚Äôs algorithm takes personalized recommendations as to the focus of user services and carries out the overall distribution of various products and reviews on its operation.

According to the algorithm principle proposed by Byte Dance, they will characterize different users based on their interests, identity characteristics, and behavior characteristics[23]. The system will characterize the users based on the types of creators they subscribe to or topics they are interested in etc‚Ä¶ The users have the freedom to subscribe to the followers they are fond of and pay more attention to their sharing. Users who have the same preferences will form their communities and share their common interests on the platform and different communities are being formed in this process.

5.2 Communities of Practice

Communities of practice are a group of people sharing common interests or regarding a particular topic such as wealth management. They can interact frequently within the communities to deepen their knowledge in this area and learn how to perform things better[24].

Unlike the traditional approach of management, communists of practice are only responsible for themselves. Thus, self-regulation will be an important characteristic in this case. Wenger has proposed COP from three dimensions. The first dimension is about a shared and negotiated understanding of what constitutes a joint enterprise and the relationships of mutual accountability [25][26].

The second point is about the mutual engagement which can define modes for both diverse and complex situations. The third point is about a shared repertoire that is embodied in a different language, routines, artifacts, tools, stories, and styles [27][25]. The importance of these three dimensions indicates the reciprocity and mutuality in communist practice in terms of regulation.

Identifying the communities is important and can also help to enhance the strategic development of the organizations [28][29]. Social media such as Dou yin should pay more attention to the degrees of formality and trust. However, we know that the informal role of Dou yin highly increases the process of knowledge diffusion. Limiting their informality can limit the engagement of people. So social media platforms like Dou yin can create some incentives for the participants in the community by letting them understand that knowledge sharing is part of the requirements for everyone on this platform [30]. This incentive will motivate the content creator for better productions of their content which can provide a better knowledge-sharing environment.

In social media such as Dou yin, there are mainly three roles in the community: the mentor, the member, and the lurkers. In common, the influencer in Dou yin will act as a role of mentor in the core of the community. They usually put their attention on their specialty and focus on the topic in a specific area. In this way, the community will be formed with an explicit style and characteristic to attract people with similar interests. The members are the people who have an interest in the topic and at the same time own the capability and knowledge about it. Thus, they usually have more passion, and they have a higher level of engagement than the lurkers in the community. In the Dou yin platform, members are in the middle of the community of practice. They are the ones appealing to the lurkers to join the community. Lastly is about the lurkers. Lurkers are the ones who usually observe the content but do not participate directly with the community. Researchers considered the lurkers as a passive form of participation to allow the online content, especially in Dou yin pass to the other users[31], [32]. They are of a significant role in passing the knowledge from different communities as they possess more time and energy for this activity.

5.3 Virtual sense of community

A virtual community is a social network consisting of people who can connect through specific social media such as Dou yin. It is usually dispersed geographically, different from the original sense of communities. In this virtual community like Dou yin, it offers the advantage of instant information exchange, also the response time can be very fast. Also, users can choose to access many discussion groups to form different specialized relationships according to their interests. These online communities can give users a feeling of membership and belongings where they will feel welcomed and received support from each other[33].

For a virtual community, the first factor is having a common domain of interest. An accepted and bounded domain of interest must exist to create and maintain a sense of community. For example, if a user is interested in the topic of financial management, he or she can join the corresponding community to accept investment knowledge. Without a certain domain, the users in Dou yin will be lost in the large content of distribution. The second factor is having Continuous interaction, it will bring all the members together and strengthen the bonds between each other and with the influencer. It will nourish emotional attachment and social recognition and make the connection stronger. The third factor is the emotional support, interaction among community members will build into an emotional gain for all members. The better emotional support a member attains, the stronger the sense of community will be [34] Successful virtual communities will give their members a safe place to express their opinions and receive emotional support from their peers. Trust among members is essential to promote knowledge sharing, as people are more willing to interact in a familiar and trustable environment.

The fourth factor is the shared history and culture[35]. Virtual communities create their version of shared history that may be different from the physical communities. Another aspect apart from shared history is the shared culture. Virtual communities with a well-established culture will set specific expectations to let their members abide by. The use of terms or language may reveal a person to be a member of the community or an outsider. So, combining the history and the culture of the virtual community should both be counted. The fifth factor is about the virtually constructed identity. The reputation, status, and role of a virtual persona are almost as important as the physical characters being established in a sense of community. Thus, in Dou yin identity should be revealed, especially for the KOL in which they can build trust and more engagement with members of the community. These five elements are crucial to maintaining a successful ongoing sustainable virtual community with active engagement.

7 Conclusion

In the questionnaire, question no.13 in the appendix, more than half of the people agree that regulators should continue to strengthen video audits related to financial knowledge sharing on social media. Thus, in Dou yin, the platform must set rules and regulations for the influencers to follow especially on topics related to investment. Social media platforms should ensure that the information shared in the community is rationalized instead of misleading or induced information to the public. Punishment and reward mechanisms should both exist in the system, and each performs its functions and duties.

This paper aims to enhance the investment awareness of the Chinese citizens by promoting knowledge sharing about wealth management on social media such as Dou yin. At the same time, they can also better understand the economic development in society and be the master of their own life. The paper also mentions the factors that the social media platform, the influencers, and the users must understand to make knowledge sharing more effective in the virtual community.

There are also limitations in this report, the collected data of the sample size is not large enough to represent the whole population of the Chinese citizens. Also, among the predictors, the variables can exist in interdependent relations with each other which may cause bias in the result.
